# Story Brief — The Guardian

---

## 1. Story Context

- **Genre**: Supernatural Horror — Protective ghost story with emotional core
- **Epoch**: Contemporary (2010s). No specific technology markers; modern suburban setting
- **Geographic location**: Suburban town in Northern United States. Cold climate with icy winters (black ice on roads)
- **Cultural context**: American suburban middle class. Single-family home, small funeral, domestic holidays and meals

---

## 2. Visual Style

- **Graphic style**: Antique Intaglio Print / Deep-etched Lithograph with Tenebrist Chiaroscuro
- **Classification**: ⬛ MONOCHROMATIC
- **Lighting**: Tenebrist Chiaroscuro — light sculpted from darkness (subtraction method). Volumetric light for smoke, steam, dust motes
- **Texture & Grain**: Heavy Plate Grain, Drypoint burr, Aquatint tonal gradients. No clean digital gradients
- **Line work**: Shadows composed of tight cross-hatching, scumbling, and rough charcoal strokes
- **Perspective**: Cine Noir wide-angle / Expressionist forced perspective
- **Palette**: Strictly Monochrome, High-contrast Sepia, or Charcoal soot. No secondary colors
- **Detail level**: High texture, low sharpness. Must look like an old physical artifact, not a digital render
- **Forbidden**: Clean lines, flat vector styles, smooth 3D lighting

---

## 3. Characters

### The Narrator (Ghost) — Protagonist

- **Name/Role**: Unknown name. The Ghost / The Guardian
- **Approximate age**: Indeterminate — died as an adult, now exists as an entity
- **Silhouette**: Amorphous humanoid figure. Recognizably person-shaped but NOT human. No mouth. Features are suggested, not defined — like a figure emerging from smoke or ink
- **Form**: Solid core with edges that dissolve into the surrounding darkness. Not translucent — more like a shape whose boundaries bleed into the environment
- **Skin texture**: Smooth, featureless where visible. Described as ice-cold
- **Distinguishing features**: Absence of mouth is the key identifier. Face is a smooth surface with only the suggestion of eyes — dark hollows or faint points of light. Hands are more defined than the rest (needs to hold objects — cradle, knife)
- **Clothing silhouette**: Clothes from the night of the accident — jacket, pants — visibly damaged. Torn fabric, crumpled shapes, asymmetric silhouette from impact damage. The clothing anchors the figure as "once human"
- **Contrast**: Very dark. The Ghost is predominantly shadow, with select areas catching light (hands, the upper face area, the edges of torn clothing)
- **Personality in one line**: Gentle, lonely, protective — a guardian who found family in death

### Jack — Father

- **Name/Role**: Jack, father of Lily, husband of Lori
- **Approximate age**: Early-to-mid 30s
- **Silhouette**: Robust, broad-shouldered. Medium-tall. Square build — solid and dependable
- **Hair style**: Short, slightly messy. Practical, no-fuss cut
- **Skin texture**: Somewhat weathered, with early lines of fatigue. Face of a father who sleeps little
- **Distinguishing features**: Wide jaw, prominent brows. Recognizable by the solidity of his form
- **Default expression**: Serious but not hard. Gaze of contained worry
- **Clothing silhouette and contrast**: Flannel shirt or henley, jeans. Loose, casual, suburban middle-class silhouette. Medium contrast (neither very dark nor very light)
- **Relationship to protagonist**: The man who accidentally killed the Ghost. Now unknowing beneficiary of the Ghost's protection. Accepts the Ghost's presence

### Lori — Mother

- **Name/Role**: Lori, mother of Lily, wife of Jack
- **Approximate age**: Early 30s
- **Silhouette**: Medium build, medium height. Not fragile — has a quiet firmness
- **Hair style**: Wavy, shoulder-length. Sometimes pulled into a messy bun. Medium volume — recognizable by wavy texture
- **Skin texture**: Smooth, soft. Appears younger than Jack
- **Distinguishing features**: Expressive eyes, defined cheekbones. Her face communicates emotion strongly
- **Default expression**: Warm by default, but with underlying tension — someone who has lived through something difficult (the ex)
- **Clothing silhouette and contrast**: Oversized sweater or cardigan over t-shirt, comfortable pants. Soft, enveloping silhouette. Light contrast (lighter clothing than Jack)
- **Relationship to protagonist**: Wife of the man who killed the Ghost. Mother of Lily. Has accepted the Ghost as family

### Lily — Daughter

- **Name/Role**: Lily, daughter of Jack and Lori
- **Approximate age**: Newborn in early scenes, ~8 years old in final scenes
- **Silhouette**: Small and petite. Thin, with the fragility of an 8-year-old child
- **Hair style**: Long and straight, loose. Falls below her shoulders — her most recognizable visual trait. The long hair creates a silhouette that contrasts with the adults
- **Skin texture**: Smooth, unmarked. Childhood softness
- **Distinguishing features**: Long hair and small stature. In compositions with adults, always the smallest figure with the softest contours
- **Default expression**: Curious, open. A child who grew up with something supernatural and accepts it naturally
- **Clothing silhouette and contrast**: Pajamas or simple dress. Clean, light silhouette. Light contrast — the brightest figure in compositions
- **Relationship to protagonist**: Born the day the Ghost died. The Ghost sees her as his own flesh and blood. The emotional anchor of the story

### The Ex — Antagonist

- **Name/Role**: Lori's ex-partner
- **Approximate age**: 30s
- **Silhouette**: Tall and wiry. Angular — everything about him is sharp and tense. Contrasts with Jack's square robustness
- **Hair style**: Shaved or very short. Visible skull — aggressive silhouette, no softness
- **Skin texture**: Tight over bones. Visible veins on arms and neck. Neglected appearance
- **Distinguishing features**: Forward-leaning posture, like someone always about to lunge. Raised shoulders, tension in neck
- **Default expression**: Clenched jaw, narrowed eyes. Contained fury spilling over
- **Clothing silhouette and contrast**: Dark, tight. Jacket or hoodie. Dark and angular silhouette — the visual opposite of the family. Very dark contrast
- **Relationship to protagonist**: Threat to the Ghost's adopted family. The catalyst for the Ghost's violent protective act

### The Boss — Minor Character

- **Name/Role**: The Ghost's former boss
- **Approximate age**: 40s–50s
- **Silhouette**: Only appears at the funeral. Background figure, no detailed design needed
- **Note**: Minimal visual presence. Can be represented as a generic dark-suited figure standing apart from the family group

## 6. Story Fragments

### Fragment 1 — The Accident

> Morí hace ocho años.
>
> No fue particularmente trágico. Ni inusual. Solo un accidente de auto. No culpo al hombre que me atropelló. Iba con exceso de velocidad porque su esposa estaba en trabajo de parto, y había hielo negro en la carretera. Perdió el control del coche y yo perdí la vida.
>
> No fue su culpa. Lo sé. No soy cruel. No soy vengativo.
>
> Si acaso, soy lo contrario.

**Visual note**: Night road. Black ice. Headlights cutting through darkness. The moment of impact or its immediate aftermath. Wide-angle perspective emphasizing the empty, frozen road. Volumetric light from headlights through steam/breath.

### Fragment 2 — The Funeral

> Verás, ya no tenía familia y había perdido a mis pocos amigos por esa época. Cuando llegó el momento de mi funeral, las únicas personas que asistieron fueron mi jefe y la familia que me mató. La esposa sostenía a su hija recién nacida contra su pecho.
>
> Odiaba a mi jefe, y el cementerio era terriblemente solitario, así que seguí a la familia hasta su casa.

**Visual note**: Desolate cemetery. Very few figures around a grave. Lori holding newborn Lily against her chest. Jack beside her. The Boss standing apart. The Ghost's amorphous silhouette watching from a distance or emerging from the grave area. Oppressive emptiness. Heavy cross-hatching in the sky.

### Fragment 3 — Rocking the Cradle

> Lily bien podría haber sido de mi propia carne y sangre. Era dulce, brillante y tan, tan pequeña. Tenía problemas para dormir si alguien no mecía su cuna y sus padres estaban tan cansados. Después de que la acostaban, era fácil para mí mecer su cuna por ella. Yo no me cansaba. Podía ayudarla.

**Visual note**: Baby's room at night. The Ghost's dark, amorphous form beside a cradle, one defined hand resting on or pushing the cradle. Soft volumetric light from a nightlight or moonlight through a window. Intimate, tender scene. The contrast between the entity's dark mass and the small, light cradle is the emotional core. Forced perspective looking slightly down at the cradle.

### Fragment 4 — Part of the Family

> Con el paso de los años, Jack y Lori se dieron cuenta de que no estaban solos en la casa. No tardaron mucho en conectar mi funeral con el momento en que aparecí. Y como nunca fui malintencionado, no tenían miedo ni estaban enojados.
>
> Empezaron a encender velas en el aniversario del día de mi muerte. Dejaban una silla vacía en las comidas y en las festividades. Realmente sentí que… era un miembro de la familia.

**Visual note**: Family dinner table. Jack, Lori, and Lily (~5-6 years old) seated. One empty chair with a lit candle nearby or on the table. The Ghost's silhouette faintly visible in/around the empty chair — present but not fully materialized. Warm candlelight creates dramatic chiaroscuro. The most emotionally warm scene — light dominates more than in other fragments.

### Fragment 5 — The Break-In

> Alguien está intentando forzar la puerta.
>
> Es el ex de Lori. Está obsesionado. Está furioso. Va a hacerle daño a la familia. A mi familia.

**Visual note**: Front door from inside. The door bulging, cracking, or the lock being forced. The Ex's angular silhouette visible through a window or the gap of a breaking door. Expressionist forced perspective — the door looming large, the hallway stretching. Maximum tension. Deep shadows. The family somewhere behind, Lily perhaps clutching Lori.

### Fragment 6 — The Knife

> La cuestión con los fantasmas es que, cuantas más ofrendas recibes, más fuerte te vuelves. Había estado disfrutando de velas, baratijas e incluso algún que otro alimento durante los últimos cinco años. Gracias a eso, era fuerte.
>
> El cuchillo se siente cálido en mi mano. Una descarga de calor contra el hielo de mi piel.
>
> Lori, Jack y Lily son mi familia. Me importan. Y no se van a unir a mí todavía.

**Visual note**: The Ghost fully manifested, standing between the intruder and the family. One defined hand gripping a kitchen knife — the knife catches light, a bright slash against the dark form. The Ghost's silhouette is larger here, empowered by years of offerings. Wide-angle from low perspective looking up at the Ghost. The family behind, the threat ahead. Maximum contrast — the Ghost is the darkest mass, the knife blade is the brightest point.
